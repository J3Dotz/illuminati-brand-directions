Red becomes structural: used for full-bleed sections, catalogue covers, exhibition panels, and packaging. A deeper red (#5C0D0F) provides layering depth without introducing a second hue family.

02   Colour System
Near-black with a red undertone carries the ground · Lacquer is the only warmth in the system
Ground — Near-Black Red#2B0405 · CMYK 0 81 78 88
Primary — Warm White#F5F0EB · CMYK 0 2 4 4
Accent — Lacquer#8E1315 · CMYK 0 87 85 44
Rule#1C0203 · CMYK 0 79 76 93
Muted Text#A8645A · CMYK 0 41 47 34
Surface — Ink#0E0B0A · CMYK 0 6 8 95

Lacquer at full saturation as a ground colour left nothing for the red to play against — every panel read at the same volume. A near-black ground with a red undertone (#2B0405) now carries the space, giving Lacquer room to function as a genuine accent: on the wordmark, on rules, on the case-study headline. This is the deepest version of the system — Lacquer reads as the only warmth in the room rather than competing with its own ground. Note: Lacquer-on-ground contrast here is decorative-grade only (logotype, large display text) — body copy and functional text stay in Warm White throughout.
